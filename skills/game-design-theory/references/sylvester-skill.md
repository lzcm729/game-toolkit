# Skill (Tynan Sylvester)

From "Designing Games: A Guide to Engineering Experiences"

## Core Concepts

### Depth
**Deep games** create meaningful play at high skill levels. Players can study them for decades (chess, poker, StarCraft).

**Shallow games** (tic-tac-toe) become boring once understood. A solved game is worthless—no uncertainty, no victory, no defeat.

### Skill Ceiling
The level of skill beyond which there is no way to improve performance.

- **Limitlessly deep:** Perfect player is better than any human (chess with computer assistance)
- **Medium ceiling:** Perfect player better than normal, but reachable (Assassin's Creed II combat)
- **Low ceiling:** Easy to execute perfect play (tic-tac-toe)

### Accessibility
**Accessible games** create meaningful play at low skill levels.

**Skill Barrier:** The lower limit of skill below which a game is unplayable (e.g., FPS requires learning controls).

### Skill Range
The range of skill levels at which a game presents meaningful challenge.

| Game | Skill Range |
|------|-------------|
| Tic-tac-toe | Narrow (left side only) |
| StarCraft II single-player | Wide (tutorial to mastery, but not limitless) |
| StarCraft II multiplayer | Wide AND deep (limitlessly deep) |

**Widest skill range = both accessible AND deep.** This is one of a designer's greatest tests.

## Stretching Skill Range

### Reinvention
Deep games **reinvent themselves** as player skill increases.

**Unreal Tournament progression:**
1. **Manual:** Learning to aim
2. **Situational:** Map control, tactical advantage
3. **Tracking:** Real-time mental map of all players
4. **Mental:** Psychological trickery, predicting opponent's mind

**Three characteristic reinventions:**
- **Manual:** Interface mastery (how to shoot)
- **Situational:** When/where/who (most players live here)
- **Mental:** Concentration, prediction, manipulation (expert level, pokerlike)

### Elastic Challenges
Permit different degrees of success/failure.

**Darts example:**
- Just bull's-eye = too hard for most
- Concentric rings = everyone has challenging but achievable goal

**Hitman:** Just kill the target (easy) vs. Silent Assassin rating (hard). Whole spectrum in between.

**Multiple degrees of failure too:**
- Missed jump → character grabs edge
- Ally attacked → incapacitated (revivable), not dead

### Training
Good training is **invisible**.

- Thread into narrative (Call of Duty 4 obstacle course)
- Turn into elastic challenges (same course is also a time trial)
- Skip when unnecessary (test players, train adaptively)

### Emotional Life Support
Before players surmount skill barrier, keep experience alive with triggers that don't require skill:
- Sublime art
- Fascinating characters
- Social joking with buddies
- Music, tech demos

**BioShock example:** Plane crash, lighthouse, art deco chamber with philosophical banner—all before learning any complex controls.

### Difficulty Modification

| Type | How It Works |
|------|--------------|
| **Explicit** | Easy/Medium/Hard selection |
| **Adaptive** | Silently adjust based on performance (works best when hidden) |
| **Implicit** | Player chooses strategy matching skill (Team Fortress 2 classes) |

## Handling Failure

**Do not punish the player himself.** Loading screens, replaying sections, grinding—these are petty punishments.

**Better alternatives:**
- Punish the player CHARACTER (shot, impoverished, tormented)
- Deny success, create small setbacks (lose some resources, miss content)
- Keep play moving forward on a new path

**Good examples:**
- **StarCraft II:** Failed attack = lost units, rebuild and try again
- **Super Meat Boy:** Death within seconds, respawn within one second
- **Dwarf Fortress:** Collapse is entertaining in itself

### Failure Traps
When player is locked into guaranteed failure for extended time.

**Examples:**
- Soccer team far behind in points
- Racing game far behind the group
- Linear game stuck on one challenge

**Solutions:**
- Elastic failure conditions (fallback weapon, minimum health recharge)
- Surrender mechanisms
- Hints, difficulty adjustment, alternative challenges

## Key Mantras

- "Players will try their hardest to solve a game, but will hate the designer if they ever succeed."
- "The most invisible training is the unnecessary training that never happens."
- "Do not punish the player himself for failure. Find other ways to create suspense."
