# The Lenses of Game Design (Jesse Schell)

From "The Art of Game Design: A Book of Lenses" (3rd Edition)

## Core Concept

**Lenses** are perspectives that help designers examine their games from different angles. Each lens poses questions that reveal hidden aspects of the game experience.

## Key Lens Details

### #1 The Lens of Emotion
> "People may forget what you said, but they'll never forget how you made them feel." —Maya Angelou

- What emotions would I like my player to experience? Why?
- What emotions are players having when they play now? Why?
- How can I bridge the gap between current emotions and desired emotions?

### #2 The Lens of Essential Experience
- What experience do I want the player to have?
- What is essential to that experience?
- How can my game capture that essence?

### #9 The Lens of the Elemental Tetrad
The four basic elements:
1. **Mechanics** - Rules and procedures
2. **Story** - Sequence of events
3. **Aesthetics** - Look, sound, feel
4. **Technology** - Medium enabling the game

All four are equally important and must work in harmony.

### #11 The Lens of Unification
- What is my theme?
- Am I using every means possible to reinforce that theme?

### #12 The Lens of Resonance
- What is it about my game that feels powerful and special?
- When I describe my game, what ideas get people really excited?
- What instincts about this game am I following?

**Two types of themes:**
- **Experience-based themes** - About delivering a certain experience
- **Truth-based themes** - About expressing a deep human truth

### #15 The Lens of the Eight Filters
Every design must pass all eight:
1. Does this game **feel right**?
2. Will the intended **audience** like this game enough?
3. Is this a **well-designed** game?
4. Is this game **novel** enough?
5. Will this game **sell**?
6. Is it **technically possible** to build this game?
7. Does this game meet our **social/community** goals?
8. Do the **playtesters** enjoy this game enough?

### #21 The Lens of Flow (Csikszentmihalyi)
Flow state requires:
- Clear goals
- No distractions
- Direct feedback
- Continuously challenging

### #40 The Lens of Triangularity
A pattern where players choose between:
- Safe action with small reward
- Risky action with large reward

Creates meaningful tension and interesting decisions.

### #49 The Lens of Elegance
**Elegance** = maximum output from minimum elements

Ask: What elements serve multiple purposes?

---

## Using the Lenses

1. **Pick a lens** relevant to your current design challenge
2. **Ask the questions** honestly
3. **Listen to the answers** - even uncomfortable ones
4. **Iterate** based on what you learn
5. **Combine lenses** for deeper insight

> "The lenses are not rules - they are questions. There is no right answer. The right answer is the one that makes your game better."
